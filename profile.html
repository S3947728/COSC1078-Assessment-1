What do you think the tests mean for you?
I think these tests are a terrible way of measuring the capability and skills of an individual, as for most of these tests I answered neutral due to being a person that can change depending on context and scenario rather than a static behavior. If I was to interpret my results, I believe they mean that I’m a flexible individual when it comes to work according to my Big Five traits, as I can work with a variety of individuals and maintain social cohesion as agreeableness is my biggest trait. The learning style and my personality results indicate that I learn through doing things, which I interpret as being able to work in a team that is more hands on in project development in the front-end of business, rather than back-end.

How do you think these results may influence your behavior in a team?
I doubt they’d greatly affect my behavior in a team context as I’d place more emphasis on professionalism and people’s ability to work with others regardless of if personalities clash or I have personal biases for disliking an individual in a team. The only takeaway I’d use is if I was to be trained for a position in a team and I’d need to ask the mentor to adapt their teachings to my preferred learning style.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?
When forming a team, personalities and the ability to cooperate and work together would be priority #1 in my opinion. As for the personality tests, a balanced set of introverts and extroverts to cover weaknesses of other individuals in a team would make the most sense. However, I think placing people with similar learning styles together could also be beneficial, as they could all teach other their missing skills despite differing personalities.
